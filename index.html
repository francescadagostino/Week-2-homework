Cincinnati, Ohio

Background

Cincinnati is a city located in southwest Ohio right along the Ohio River that separates Ohio and Kentucky. It originally was named Losantiville and nicknamed Porkopolis because of the mass amount of pork that was produced there. It eventually became Cincinnati after the Roman soldier Cincinnatus. The city is laid between the seven hills that make up the local geography.

What Cincinnat is Known for

Cincinnat is known for a variety of things including the infamous Skyline Chili, the ice cream brand Graeters that was founded in the 19th century, and their major league baseball team the Cincinnati Reds. Cincinnati is stereotyped as an antiquated city, in fact, Mark Twain famously said that when the world ends he wants to be in Cincinnati because they are twenty years behind the times. Famous people from Cincinnati include president William Howard Taft, writer Harriet Beecher Stowe, and baseball player Pete Rose.
